## QUORA
**Answer this question:** What is the most common financial mistake people make without realizing it?

The most common financial mistake I see is prioritizing the wrong debt simultaneously. People will diligently build an emergency fund or savings account while carrying high-interest debt—sometimes without even realizing the math doesn't work.

I did exactly this. I was contributing regularly to a savings account earning 0.01% annual interest while carrying a credit card balance charging 22.99% APR. For two years. The irony was that I felt responsible about saving money.

Here's why this happens: personal finance advice often treats all debt and all savings the same way. Build an emergency fund. Save consistently. These aren't bad principles, but they ignore the actual leverage in your numbers.

The fix is brutal math: If you're earning 0.01% on savings and paying 23% on debt, the math is screaming that debt elimination is your actual leverage point. That's not advice—that's just numbers.

Most people don't sit down and actually look at the rates side-by-side. They follow generic rules instead of looking at their specific situation. The financial moves that move the needle are rarely the comfortable ones.
